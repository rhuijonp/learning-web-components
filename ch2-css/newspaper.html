Floats

Floats behave as balloons trapped in a room in some respect. A css box is the balloon, and you can tie a leash to it so you can pull it. In css you can only pull right or left, so all the balloons that you pull to the left will bunch up in the left side of their parent container, and the same goes for the ones you pull to the right, except these pile up on the right side.

Just like balloons, they do not occupy the same space, so if you have 5 balloons, and you pull them all to the right, they will line up one behind the other.

If you pull some of the balloons to the right and some to the left, as expected, they will bunch up to the right and left, leaving empty space in the middle, if the area is large enough.

What happens when you have enough balloons to cover the entire top of the room they are in? They will start to go under each other, but they will still be pulled right or left.

Now, if there a balloon or balloons you do not want to see floating right or left? Perhaps you tie them to something, in the case of css boxes, you tell you do not want them floating by using "none".

Lastly, if you do not want any more balloons floating in the space you are? You can tell put a barrier, so that no more balloons are allowed to float beyond that barrier. In css, that is accomplish by using "clear both", meaning, do not allow any more balloons floating left or right.

Of course, you can keep floating balloons in another area of the room, or you can go to another room.
